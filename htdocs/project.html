RESEARCH

Research forms an esential part of the conservation actions on Koh Karang. By monotoring the eco-system around the island, we can evaluate the impact of our conservational actions and build cases for the further development of the Marine National Park. Assessing biodiversity will add to the scientific knowledge of the area.

  • Specimen quantity and diversity surveys
  • Monitoring of seagrass, mangrove and coral growth
  • Identification and monitoring of key stone species such as seahorses in order evaluate the health and quality of the marine eco-system

ZONING

Zonation is the process of allocating spatial and temporal designations to defined areas within a larger region. It is a key management tool in the creation of MFMAs. This specific method defines areas to be used for particular activities such as research, education, fishing, tourism and the protection of key habitats and nursery grounds of marine species. Zoning has three major potential benefits: it resolves the issue of widespread IUU fishing methods; it protects critical marine stocks, vulnerable species and habitats; and it reduces conflicts between commercial and subsistence fishers.
