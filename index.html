Why

Facilitating a sustainable symbiosis between Human and Nature.

How

Embracing the environment through locally embedded solutions that support the most vulnerable: shallow waters and its local fishermen.

What

Create an inclusive platform where innovative ideas and practices can be explored that increase the quality of life for both man and animal.

A Creative Portfolio Template

All around the world we are experiencing a rapid decline of our marine ecosystems. Due to unsustainable and often illegal fishing practices, the pressure on marine life is set for disaster. Especially in Cambodia ; where environmental pressure, a lack of legal enforcement and scarcity of resources are added to the test, there is a direct need for action. So it is here that we will do what we do best… Embrace. We aim to find solutions in an embrace between MAN and NATURE, so that a sustainable symbiosis can be reestablished.

At embrace we stand for our consequences. We aim to dive deeper into the problem to figure out how to provide support at the base of the problem. We believe in a positive – solution based approach.

So, what is the real problem of Cambodia’s marine life? For us, it is not the illegal fishing practices, neither can we blame the unsatisfiable market for fish, and we definitely don’t just blame the environment either. For sure they are viable causes… but not the reason! Our problem is Scarcity (potential link to scarcity principle). Scarcity limits the possibility of better solutions. It drives the fishermen to fish illegally, it limits us to invest in protected areas and it creates a hungry market. In fact, it is the base of all negative effects.

But, luckily, our oceans contain a vastly abundant potential and with the right motivation, we can find the ideas that allow our local heroes to tap into this in a sustainable way. We will use nature to solve nature’s problems and allow fisherman to collaborate in the process.

Want to know what exactly that entails? We invite you to join our Embrace and discover from past to future.

  • What happens on Koh Karang, doesn’t stay on Koh Karang…

    Embrace has chosen Koh Karang as the center of its spirit; underlining the rationale that if it’s possible on (or underneath) a small 200x80m island without running water, inhabitants or protection, it is possible anywhere. We aim to develop ideas that benefit all and put them to the test. Koh Karang will therefor become a platform where the local community, NGOs, corporations, tourists and the government can interact on pressing social and environmental issues. We aim to create an oasis where ideas can grow, a place where we see problems as opportunities.

    By creating a common fertile ground we aim to inspire the entire Kep region with alternatives. By inviting and involving the local communities, we aim to foster understanding of the important value of our marine life and how it affects us all.
